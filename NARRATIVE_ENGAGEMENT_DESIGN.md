# KAYFABE Narrative Engagement Design
## Cognitive Reframing Through Wrestling Mechanics

**Date:** 2026-01-17
**Purpose:** Design intentional, psychologically-grounded challenge, leaderboard, and faction systems
**Core Thesis:** How you narrate your life determines your experience. KAYFABE makes narrative reframing engaging through wrestling RPG mechanics.

---

## Part 1: The Psychology Foundation

### 1.1 Core Psychological Principles

**1. Narrative Identity Theory (Dan McAdams)**
- People construct identity through life stories
- Changing the narrative changes self-concept
- Stories have heroes, villains, victories, setbacks
- **KAYFABE Application:** Users are the protagonists; they choose the genre (Face vs Heel)

**2. Cognitive Reframing (CBT)**
- Same event, different interpretations
- Reframing negative â†’ empowering
- Practice creates neural pathways
- **KAYFABE Application:** Promos are structured reframes; daily practice rewires thinking

**3. Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)**
- Autonomy: Choice over narrative
- Competence: Progress tracking (XP, streaks)
- Relatedness: Community validation (pops, challenges)
- **KAYFABE Application:** All three needs met through gamification

**4. Implementation Intentions (Peter Gollwitzer)**
- "When X happens, I will Y"
- Pre-planning responses to triggers
- Increases follow-through by 300%
- **KAYFABE Application:** Goals are IF-THEN plans; challenges make them social

---

### 1.2 Wrestling as Narrative Framework

**Why Wrestling Works for Cognitive Reframing:**

1. **Clear Archetypes:**
   - Face = Empowering, growth-minded, hero narrative
   - Heel = Challenging, realistic, antihero narrative
   - Both valid, both useful

2. **Performance Mindset:**
   - "I'm playing a character" creates psychological distance
   - Easier to try new narratives when it's "just a promo"
   - Reduces fear of judgment

3. **Stakes & Drama:**
   - Life is more engaging when framed as a storyline
   - "I'm training for the championship" > "I'm going to the gym"
   - Creates emotional investment in habits

4. **Progression System:**
   - Rookie â†’ Jobber â†’ Main Eventer â†’ Champion = Hero's Journey
   - Mirrors actual personal development
   - Makes growth visible and gamified

## Part 2: Intentional Challenge Design

### 2.1 Challenge Philosophy

**Current Problem:**
Most habit apps have boring challenges:
- "Drink 8 glasses of water for 30 days"
- No narrative
- No emotional connection
- No cognitive reframing

**KAYFABE Challenges:**
Every challenge is a **narrative arc** with:
- **Story:** The "why" (motivation, meaning)
- **Stakes:** What you're fighting for
- **Obstacles:** Expected setbacks, reframed
- **Payoff:** Not just XP, but identity shift

**Category 3: Cognitive Distortion Challenges**

**Concept:** Target specific thinking patterns (CBT-based)

**Example: "The All-or-Nothing Assassin" (14 days)**

**Target:** All-or-nothing thinking ("If I'm not perfect, I failed")

**Challenge Structure:**
```
Goal: Complete ANY version of your goal for 14 days straight

Twist: You define 3 versions of success:
- Championship Performance (100%): Your ideal
- Main Event Performance (70%): Solid effort
- Opening Match (40%): You showed up

Rules:
- ANY completion counts (no zeros)
- Track which level you hit each day
- Write promo reframing "less than perfect" as valid

Daily Promo Prompt Examples:
- "Today was an Opening Match performance, but that means..."
- "I didn't hit Championship level, but I reframe this as..."
- "All-or-nothing thinking wanted me to quit. Instead, I..."

Leaderboard Twist:
- NOT ranked by Championship days
- Ranked by CONSISTENCY (14 days straight at any level)
- Shows "Perfect is the enemy of done"

Rewards:
- "Consistency Champion" badge
- +750 XP
- Unlock "Progress Not Perfection" gimmick tagline
```

**Why This Works:**
- Directly targets cognitive distortion
- Makes imperfection part of the game
- Builds resilience
- Leaderboard rewards behavior change, not perfection
- Users internalize "showing up > being perfect"

## Part 7: Ethical Guardrails

### 7.1 Preventing Toxic Gamification

**Risks:**
1. **Addiction:** Endless scrolling, compulsive posting
2. **Comparison:** "I'm not as good as them"
3. **Performativity:** Posting for validation, not growth
4. **Toxicity:** Faction wars, harassment

**Mitigations:**

**1. Usage Limits**
```
- Max 3 posts per day (prevents compulsive posting)
- Feed refreshes every 5 minutes, not real-time (reduces FOMO)
- "Take a Break" reminder after 30min in feed
- Weekly screen time report: "You spent X hours. Is this serving you?"
```

**2. Hidden Metrics**
```
- Follower counts NOT displayed publicly (reduces comparison)
- Total XP hidden from others after 25K (reduces status-seeking)
- Private "Are you posting for you or for pops?" check-in
```

**3. Anti-Harassment**
```
- Can't tag users in posts without permission
- Factions can't @ other factions (prevents wars)
- Report system with human review
- Auto-mute after 3 reports (temp, pending review)
```

**4. Mental Health Safeguards**
```
- Partnered therapists available (in-app referrals)
- Crisis resources always accessible (top nav)
- AI flags concerning language (self-harm mentions)
- "Talk to a human" button in every challenge
```
